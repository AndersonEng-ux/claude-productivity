Methodology

How Multipliers Are Calculated

Each entry represents a named deliverable produced with Claude assistance. Entries without a tangible output (meetings, phone calls, email routing) are excluded. Multipliers are derived from three independent evidence layers, with conservative lower-bound estimates applied.
Layer 1 — Internal Baseline
QuickBooks Time (TSheets) entries from 2024, pre-Claude. Matched by jobcode + keyword similarity to establish historical duration for comparable tasks. Weighted 2x when available.
Layer 2 — AEC Industry Benchmarks
PSMJ Resources (pursuit time studies), Deltek Clarity Report (utilization benchmarks), SMPS Foundation (proposal/SOQ time surveys), ACEC benchmarks, RSMeans labor data, ENR productivity indices.
Layer 3 — Published AI Research
Harvard/BCG (40% faster analytical tasks, 2023), MIT/Stanford (37% faster professional writing), GitHub/Microsoft (55% faster coding), McKinsey (25-50% dev productivity), NBER (14% support productivity), Anthropic SWE-bench (2x coding tasks).
Human-equivalent hours = weighted average of internal baseline (2x weight) + industry benchmark. When no internal baseline exists, industry + research benchmarks are used. All multipliers represent the conservative lower bound of the evidence range.
